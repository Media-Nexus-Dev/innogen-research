SNAP-8 (Acetyl Octapeptide-3) is a synthetic peptide derived from the N-terminal of the SNAP-25 protein. In research settings, it is investigated as a topical compound that competes with SNAP-25 for a position in the SNARE complex, thereby modulating the release of catecholamines such as acetylcholine at the neuromuscular junction.

By destabilizing the formation of the SNARE complex, SNAP-8 has been studied for its potential to reduce the depth of facial wrinkles caused by repeated muscle contraction, particularly around the forehead and eyes.
